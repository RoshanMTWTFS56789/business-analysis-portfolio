# ERP Transformation & Business Information Systems Project

## Project Overview
This project was completed as part of the MS834 Business Information Systems module within the MSc Business Analysis and Consulting programme at the University of Strathclyde.

The project evaluated traditional Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) architecture and compared it with emerging ERP technologies, particularly SAP HANA and blockchain-enabled ERP systems. The analysis focused on how newer technologies can address limitations in traditional ERP systems, including data latency, scalability, integration complexity, transparency and flexibility.

## Business Problem
Traditional ERP systems support centralised data, standardised processes and enterprise-wide coordination. However, they can become difficult to scale, slow to upgrade and limited in real-time decision-making. This project explored how organisations can assess whether technologies such as SAP HANA and blockchain are suitable for ERP transformation.

## Objectives
- Evaluate traditional ERP architecture and its limitations.
- Compare traditional ERP with SAP HANA and blockchain-enabled ERP.
- Analyse industries likely to be disrupted by emerging ERP technologies.
- Apply the Technology-Organization-Environment framework to understand adoption factors.
- Develop recommendations for organisations considering ERP transformation.

## Methods and Frameworks Used
- ERP architecture analysis
- SAP HANA technology evaluation

- Comparative evaluation
- Industry disruption analysis

- Strategic recommendation development

## Key Findings
- Traditional ERP systems are strong for standardisation and governance but can be limited by data latency, rigid customisation and integration complexity.
- SAP HANA can improve real-time analytics, reporting speed and operational responsiveness.
- Blockchain-enabled ERP can improve transparency, traceability, auditability and smart-contract-based automation.
- ERP transformation should be phased and supported by governance controls, digital capability building and targeted high-value use cases.

## Recommendations
- Use a phased ERP transition approach instead of immediate full replacement.
- Prioritise high-value use cases such as real-time reporting, traceability, compliance automation and supply chain visibility.
- Build internal digital capability through training, governance and vendor collaboration.
- Monitor ERP innovation continuously to maintain flexibility and competitiveness.
